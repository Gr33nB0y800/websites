Early life

Gál Krisztina, known professionally as Kriszti White, was born on December 5, 1971, in Salgótarján, Hungary. From a young age, Kriszti showed an extraordinary aptitude for both music and science. Growing up in Salgótarján, a historic town in northern Hungary, she spent her formative years immersed in both her academic studies and her burgeoning passion for music.

While attending Salgótarján High School, Kriszti was not only at the top of her class academically but also made a name for herself as a talented musician. She participated in various school music events, showcasing her vocal prowess and musical abilities.

After graduating from high school with top honors, Kriszti pursued higher education, earning a degree in Chemistry from the University of Budapest. Despite her academic success, her passion for music never waned, and she continued to perform in local bands and music festivals throughout her university years.

Career

Kriszti White's career is as multifaceted as her talents. She has experienced success and acclaim in various fields, ranging from education to music.

Chemistry Teacher

After completing her studies, Kriszti briefly worked as a chemistry teacher at a high school in Monor. Her engaging teaching style and passion for science inspired her students, many of whom went on to pursue careers in chemistry and related fields.

Alleged Druglord

Rumours circulated about Kriszti's involvement in the underground drug trade, though these allegations have never been proven. Some speculate that her knowledge of chemistry and her connections in the music industry made her an ideal candidate for such activities, but Kriszti vehemently denies any involvement in illegal activities.

Accessing the 5th Dimension

Kriszti gained notoriety for her claims of accessing the 5th dimension through her psychedelic experiences. She often speaks cryptically about her spiritual journeys and the insights she has gained from them. While some dismiss her claims as mere hallucinations, others are fascinated by her exploration of consciousness and the unknown.

Rockstar Singer

In 2010, Kriszti burst onto the Hungarian music scene with her band Kriszti's Chem Lab. Their debut album, "Crystal," received critical acclaim and catapulted Kriszti to fame. Her electrifying performances and unique style of psychedelic rock captivated audiences, earning her a dedicated following.

Throughout her career, Kriszti has toured extensively, both as a solo artist and with her band. She has performed at prestigious music festivals around the world and collaborated with renowned musicians from diverse genres.

Personal life

Despite her enigmatic public persona, Gál Krisztina leads a relatively private personal life. She is known to be a devoted advocate for environmental causes, often using her platform to raise awareness about issues such as climate change and deforestation.

When she's not on stage or in the studio, Kriszti enjoys spending time in nature, practicing meditation, and exploring new ways to expand her consciousness.

Awards and Achievements

Throughout her illustrious career, Kriszti White has received numerous awards and accolades. In addition to her musical achievements, she has been recognized for her contributions to education, science, and spirituality.

In 2013, she was awarded the prestigious "Golden Microscope" for her outstanding work as a chemistry teacher. The award recognized her innovative teaching methods and dedication to inspiring the next generation of scientists.

She has also been honoured with the "Psychedelic Pioneer Award" for her groundbreaking work in the realm of psychedelic music. The award celebrates her role in pushing the boundaries of conventional music and exploring new frontiers of sound and consciousness.
